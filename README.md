# My Reflection: Mock Interview Experience
 
For my WIL preparation, I had to do a mock interview video where I answered different interview questions. The idea was to get a feel for a real interview and practise speaking more confidently.
  
I had to pick four questions from different categories and answer them properly while presenting myself in a professional way. I also needed to think about my own experiences and not just give textbook answers.

 
I chose these questions: *What are you passionate about?*, *How do you handle stress?*, *Tell me about a time you made a mistake and how you fixed it*, and *What does success mean to you?*
I prepared by really thinking about what each question meant to me personally. For the behavioural question, I tried using the STAR method to structure my answer. I didn’t memorise anything — I just focused on being honest and natural.
I did the recording with a friend who asked me the questions, and we set up a simple, quiet space with a plain background. It was a bit awkward at first being on camera, and it was also challenging to decide what to say on the spot, but preparing beforehand helped a lot.

Overall, the experience helped me feel more confident speaking in interviews. I learned how to organise my thoughts better and stay calm under pressure. It also showed me that preparation makes a big difference, and I feel more ready for real interviews now.
